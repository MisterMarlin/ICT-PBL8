• 1950s

    The concept of AI emerged with Alan Turing's work, including the Turing Test, which measures a machine's ability to exhibit human-like intelligence.

  • 1956s

    The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined at the Dartmouth Conference, marking the birth of AI as a field of study.

  • 1960s-1980s

    AI research focused on symbolic reasoning, expert systems, and early machine learning models.

  • 1990s-2000s

    AI applications expanded into fields like game playing (Deep Blue defeating chess champion Garry Kasparov) and speech recognition.

  • 2010s-Present.

    The rise of deep learning, big data, and increased computing power led to significant advancements in AI, with applications in self-driving cars, facial recognition, AI-generated art, and more.
