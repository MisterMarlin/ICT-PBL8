Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that focuses on creating intelligent machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include problem-solving, learning, reasoning, perception, language understanding, and decision-making. AI enables computers and systems to analyze data, recognize patterns, and make informed decisions with minimal human intervention.

    Timeline of AI

  • 1950s

    The concept of AI emerged with Alan Turing's work, including the Turing Test, which measures a machine's ability to exhibit human-like intelligence.

  • 1956s

    The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined at the Dartmouth Conference, marking the birth of AI as a field of study.

  • 1960s-1980s

    AI research focused on symbolic reasoning, expert systems, and early machine learning models.

  • 1990s-2000s

    AI applications expanded into fields like game playing (Deep Blue defeating chess champion Garry Kasparov) and speech recognition.

  • 2010s-Present.

    The rise of deep learning, big data, and increased computing power led to significant advancements in AI, with applications in self-driving cars, facial recognition, AI-generated art, and more.

General AI

Artificial general intelligence (AGI) refers to the hypothetical intelligence of a machine that possesses the ability to understand or learn any intellectual task that a human being can. It is a type of artificial intelligence (AI) that aims to mimic the cognitive abilities of the human brain.
AI and Machine Learning

Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence that automatically enables a machine or system to learn and improve from experience. Instead of explicit programming, machine learning uses algorithms to analyze large amounts of data, learn from the insights, and then make informed decisions.
Limited Memory AI

In AI, limited memory refers to systems that temporarily store data for making decisions. Unlike reactive machines, these systems have a short-lived or transient memory, used to improve their predictions or decisions based on recent data.
Theory of Mind AI

"Theory of mind AI" refers to the concept of developing artificial intelligence systems that can understand and model the mental states of other agents, like humans, by comprehending their thoughts, intentions, beliefs, and emotions, essentially allowing the AI to "read minds" and interact more naturally and effectively with people
